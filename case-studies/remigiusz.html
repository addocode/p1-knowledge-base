Remigiusz

Case Study·Updated 28 Jun 2026·7 min·Confidence: Medium

Remigiusz is documented as a private-owner case study because his Booking and rental data became a reference point for testing whether a Letnica/Brzeźno apartment could actually generate demand.

v6.0 Knowledge EditionCase Study

TL;DR

Case summary
  • Role: private seller / owner with a documented summer Booking and winter long-term-rental strategy.
  • Importance: provided real income evidence rather than only verbal yield claims.
  • Learning: seasonal hybrid operation can be more credible when supported by month-level documentation.
  • Investor rule: a seller story becomes stronger when documents, prices and occupancy patterns align.
  • Limitation: more months and full annual reports would still be needed for a complete underwriting model.

Evidence pattern

The Remigiusz case became valuable because it moved the discussion from opinion to documentation. The available Booking and rental records suggested a plausible operating model: short-term rental in the stronger summer months and long-term rental in winter. This created a more nuanced view of Letnica and Brzeźno demand.

Seasonality

The documents showed that summer performance can differ dramatically by month. August appeared materially stronger than May. This is typical for coastal and seasonal markets, but seeing the pattern in actual records matters more than hearing a seller describe it.

Investor lesson

Private sellers often tell stories. The investor’s job is to test whether the story produces contradictions. In this case, the pricing pattern, rental contracts and Booking records were internally coherent. That did not automatically make the apartment a buy, but it increased confidence that the operating history was not invented.
