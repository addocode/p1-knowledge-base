IT-GOA-001 — Genova Candidate

Case Studies · Updated 27 Jun 2026 · 7 min · Confidence: Low

Placeholder dossier for the next research phase: Genova.

Genova Italy Future

Executive Summary

  • IT-GOA-001 is a Genova candidate dossier, not a buy recommendation. It must remain “yellow” until title, condominium rules, building condition, operator quote, and realistic net ROI are evidenced.
  • Decision rule: no deposit before legal-title review, building checklist, and rental-compliance path are complete.
  • The trip objective is to convert unknowns into evidence: photos, documents, operator pricing, comparable listings, and red flags.

P1 Insight

P1 Insight. A candidate becomes investable only when it survives boring checks. If the file is exciting but document-light, classify it as a learning asset, not a transaction.

Asset facts needed

Item Status Evidence required
Address and floor Open Listing, cadastral plan, building visit.
Seller identity and authority Open ID/company docs, mandate, notary pre-check.
Legal title Open Deed, cadastral consistency, mortgage/encumbrance statement, notary review.
Condominium position Open House rules, recent minutes, charges, arrears, extraordinary works.
Rental operation Open AAUT/CITRA/CIN path, city-tax obligations, operator quote.

Underwriting assumptions to fill on site

  • Purchase price, agency fee, notary/tax estimate, immediate capex, furnishing replacement, and first-year reserve.
  • Base, downside, and stress ADR/occupancy in the ROI Model.
  • Management fee, cleaning fee treatment, linen cost, maintenance call-out cost, platform mix, and tourist-tax workflow.
  • Exit buyer: investor, local resident, student/worker landlord, or lifestyle buyer.

Candidate inspection checklist

  • Run the Building Check before discussing price.
  • Photograph every ceiling corner, window reveal, bathroom wall, meter, boiler, electrical panel, and common area.
  • Test water pressure, drainage smell, windows, shutters, heating/cooling, Wi-Fi options, and phone signal.
  • Ask for the condominium administrator contact and permission to request charge/works information.

Go / no-go criteria

  • Go to negotiation: clean legal path, acceptable building, written operating path, conservative net yield, and manageable capex.
  • Hold: attractive price but missing documents or operator quote.
  • No-go: title ambiguity, rental restriction, severe moisture/structure concern, unfunded extraordinary works, unsafe route, or ROI dependent on best-case assumptions.

Sources

  • Use Regione Liguria AAUT/CITRA/CIN guidance and Comune di Genova IDS guidance for rental-compliance assumptions.
  • Use Agenzia delle Entrate purchase-tax guidance for tax placeholders, then confirm with the notary.
