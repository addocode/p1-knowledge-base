Building & Unit Check

Due Diligence · Updated 27 Jun 2026 · 9 min · Confidence: High

Physical inspection framework for buildings, floors, elevators, hallways, noise and view quality.

Building Noise View

Executive Summary

  • In Genova, building risk can dominate apartment quality. Historic fabric, hills, moisture, salt air, stairs, and condominium works must be inspected before price negotiation.
  • Do not rely on fresh interiors. The common parts and envelope decide long-term capex and guest experience.

P1 Insight

P1 Insight. P1 should prefer a plain apartment in a healthy building over a photogenic unit inside a deteriorating condominium.

Physical inspection rubric

  • Facade and roof: cracks, netting, missing plaster, active works, roof access, gutters, water paths.
  • Moisture: smell, bubbling paint, salt bloom, bathroom ventilation, window condensation, ground-floor risk.
  • Structure: diagonal cracks, sloping floors, stuck doors/windows, patched beams, visible settlement.
  • Common areas: entrance, stairs, lighting, handrails, elevator, mailboxes, cleanliness, security.
  • Utilities: electrical panel, gas, boiler, heating/cooling, meters, water pressure, drains, internet availability.
  • Renovation constraints: delivery access, waste removal, contractor parking, condominium approvals, protected-building constraints.

Field-trip questions

  • When were roof, facade, elevator, electrical risers, and plumbing last repaired?
  • Are lavori straordinari approved, proposed, or disputed?
  • What is the annual condominium charge and reserve position?
  • Has any insurance claim, water leak, or structural assessment occurred recently?

Red flags

  • Scaffolding or facade netting with no budget and timeline.
  • Seller cannot provide condominium minutes or charge statements.
  • Top-floor damp, ground-floor damp, sewage smell, overloaded electrics, or non-functioning elevator.
  • Beautiful renovation that did not address ventilation, wiring, plumbing, or windows.
